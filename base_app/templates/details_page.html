All About

It was originally taken from a Latin text written by a Roman Scholar,
Sceptic and Philosopher by the name of Marcus Tullius Cicero,
who influenced the Latin language greatly.

The "filler" text we know today has been altered over the years in
fact "Lorem" isn't actually a Latin word. It is suggested that the
reason that the text starts with "Lorem" is because there was a page
break spanning the word "Do-lorem". If you a re looking for a translation
of the text, it's meaningless. The original text talks about the pain and
love involved in the pursuit of pleasure or something like that.
